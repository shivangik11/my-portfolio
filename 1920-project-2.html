GREEN FOOT
UI UX Boot Camp Project, 2021
Green Foot is an integrated system that allows people to calculate their carbon footprint simply by entering their daily actions into the system, which can then be redeemed or reduced by using the supplied advice.
Who will be the Users
The users targeted were, those who want to do something good for the world and wanted to make world a better place but are unable to do because of their busy lives and work cultures; however, this notion may enable them pursue it.
Defining the Problem
Given the facts and figures, it has become absolutely essential for each individual to keep track of their carbon release in the surroundings. Small, incremental changes make a big effect, and your daily behaviors keep adding to them.

The largest single source of global greenhouse gas emissions is the burning of coal, natural gas, and oil for electricity and heat.

Facts

Before 2030, temperatures may have risen by 1.5 degrees Celsius (2.7 degrees Fahrenheit), which is the G7 countries’ limit and the Paris Agreement’s aspirational target.

Petroleum-based fuels, mostly gasoline and diesel, provide nearly all 95% of the world’s transportation energy.

The aviation industry accounts for around 3.5 percent of all human-induced climate change.

Electricity generation and transportation are important polluters, with coal-fired power plants accounting for 20% of GHG emissions.

The digital sector contributes from 2% and 4% of worldwide GHG emissions, with chip manufacturing accounting for the majority of this.

Hypotheses
Here is the solution to this problem, taking into account the issues and facts. An app designed with the idea of calculating carbon footprints by simply inputting routine activities and modifying them with minor additions with the help of provided tips.
